About Me


My name is Mona Essaid and I am a full-stack developer from Los Angeles, CA. I am a recent graduate with a master’s degree in Software Development from the University of Glasgow in Scotland. But my undergraduate degree was not in tech - I love telling stories and solving problems creatively, so I chose to study marketing during my bachelors degree. I decided that I wanted to pursue a career in tech during the last semester of my undergraduate degree. I took a user experience design course and loved the combination of creative problem solving, psychology, and technical challenges that it offered.

After I graduated, I worked in a variety of fields with the goal of continuing to learn more about technology in my free time so I could eventually transition into more technical roles. I was able to learn more about front-end web development through the Grow with Google Scholarship that I received in 2018, and I independently studied Java and JavaScript over the years as well. I decided to go back to school to formally study software development and gain the skills I needed to change my career in 2022. I loved living and studying in a different environment, and being immersed in another culture gave me valuable perspective.

Since I came back to the US in early 2024, I have been working on personal projects, earning certifications, and fine-tuning my master’s thesis project with the goal of releasing it in early 2025. I joined the Cook Systems FastTrack program in November 2024. During the program, I have completed backend projects in Java, SpringBoot, and Node, and frontend projects with html, CSS, JavaScript, and Angular. I look forward to continuing to refine my technical skills and continuing my career in software development with Cook Systems.
